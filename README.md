# HR-Analytics-project_powerBi

data analytics project analyzing employee attrition patterns across 1,480 employees! This dashboard reveals critical insights that can help organizations reduce turnover and improve retention strategies.
### Key Findings:
16.08% Overall Attrition Rate – With 237 employees leaving, the data reveals distinct patterns worth investigating.

#### High-Risk Areas Identified:
- Young professionals (18-25) show 35.77% attrition – nearly 2x the company average
- Employees working overtime are 3x more likely to leave (30.62% vs 10.36%)
- Sales Representatives face the highest risk (39.29% attrition rate)
- Sales department struggles with 20.67% attrition vs 13.75% in R&D

#### Compensation Gap:
Employees who left earned $2,017 less on average ($4,813 vs $6,829) – suggesting competitive salary is a key retention factor.

#### Critical Insights:
- Poor work-life balance (Rating 1) correlates with 30.86% attrition
- Lower job satisfaction drives 22.87% attrition vs 11.28% for highly satisfied employees
- Shorter tenure employees (avg 5.1 years) are more likely to leave than long-term staff (7.4 years)
  
#### Actionable Recommendations:
- Implement mentorship programs for employees aged 18-25
- Review overtime policies and workload distribution
- Conduct salary benchmarking, especially for early-career roles
- Enhance work-life balance initiatives in Sales departments
- Focus retention efforts on employees with 0-3 years tenure
